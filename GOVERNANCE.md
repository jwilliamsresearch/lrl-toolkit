# Governance

`lrl-toolkit` is an open-source, community-oriented project. This document describes how decisions are made. It is intentionally lightweight for the project's early stage and will evolve.

## Roles

- **Contributors** — anyone who opens issues or pull requests.
- **Maintainers** — contributors with commit rights who review PRs, triage issues, and cut releases. Maintainers are added by consensus of existing maintainers based on sustained, high-quality contribution.
- **Language stewards** — speakers or community representatives for a specific language who advise on that language's profiles, data sources, and appropriate use. Stewards have effective veto over decisions that affect *their* language's data and models, in the spirit of the CARE principles (see [DATA_ETHICS.md](DATA_ETHICS.md)).

## Decision making

- **Everyday changes** (bug fixes, connectors, docs): lazy consensus — merge after review and no sustained objection.
- **Significant changes** (architecture, new pipeline stages, dependency shifts): open an issue for discussion first; seek explicit maintainer consensus.
- **Ethics / data-governance decisions**: require input from relevant language stewards where a specific language community is affected.

## Adding languages & data sources

New language profiles and connectors are encouraged. Where a language has an identifiable community, we actively seek a **language steward** before promoting that language to a "supported" status in the docs.

## Code of Conduct

All participation is governed by the [Code of Conduct](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md). Maintainers are responsible for enforcement.

## Contact

Use GitHub Issues for public discussion. For conduct or sensitive data-governance matters, contact the maintainers privately (see repository profile).
